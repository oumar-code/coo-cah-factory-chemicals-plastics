## 1. Regulatory Landscape Summary

Operating a polymer/plastics manufacturing facility in Nigeria involves oversight from six
distinct regulatory authorities, each with specific permit and compliance requirements.

| Authority | Full Name | Scope |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **NESREA** | National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency | Environmental permits, hazardous materials, effluent discharge |
| **NUPRC** (formerly DPR) | Nigerian Upstream Petroleum Regulatory Commission | Hydrocarbon feedstock handling permits |
| **SON** | Standards Organisation of Nigeria | Product quality certification, SONCAP import certificates |
| **NAFDAC** | National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control | Food-contact plastic products (PET bottles, food packaging) |
| **NCS** | Nigeria Customs Service | Import documentation (Form M), tariff classification |
| **Lagos State Fire Service / NFPA** | Lagos State Fire Service | Fire safety compliance for chemical and industrial storage |

### 2.2 Ongoing NESREA Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Standard | Frequency |
|-------------|----------|-----------|

| Annual environmental report | Self-monitoring report submitted to NESREA | Annual |

### 2.3 Key Environmental Parameters (ETP Discharge Limits)

| Parameter | NESREA Limit | Internal Target | Monitoring |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| pH | 6.0 – 9.0 | 6.5 – 8.5 | Daily |
| COD | ≤ 40 mg/L | ≤ 30 mg/L | Weekly |
| BOD₅ | ≤ 30 mg/L | ≤ 20 mg/L | Weekly |
| Total Suspended Solids (TSS) | ≤ 30 mg/L | ≤ 20 mg/L | Weekly |

### 4.2 SONCAP (SON Conformity Assessment Programme)

All polymer raw materials imported must carry **SONCAP certificates** obtained at the point of
origin (pre-shipment inspection). This applies to:
- PP/PE granule imports (backup source)
- PET resin imports
- Specialty additive imports

## 5. NAFDAC — Food-Contact Plastic Products

### 5.1 NAFDAC Scope

NAFDAC regulates all plastic products that come into contact with food or beverages:
- **CCH-PLS-004** — PET bottles and preforms (food and beverage contact)
- **CCH-PLS-003** — PP/PE blown film for food packaging

### 5.2 NAFDAC Registration Requirements

| Requirement | Detail | Status |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Manufacturer registration | Register factory as a food-packaging manufacturer with NAFDAC | ☐ Not started |
| Product registration | Each food-contact plastic product requires individual NAFDAC registration | ☐ Not started |
| Raw material declaration | List of all raw materials, additives, and process aids submitted to NAFDAC | ☐ Not started |
| Migration testing | Heavy metals and additive migration testing per EU 10/2011 or FDA 21 CFR | ☐ Not started |
| Acetaldehyde (AA) testing | PET bottles — AA must be < 3 ppm (NAFDAC food contact requirement) | ☐ Not started |
| Facility inspection | NAFDAC factory inspection before product registration approval | ☐ Not started |
| Annual renewal | NAFDAC registration renewal | Annual |

### 5.3 Key Food-Contact Raw Material Requirements

For NAFDAC-compliant food-contact production:
- PET resin: Must have food-contact declaration from supplier (FDA/EFSA compliance)
- Masterbatch: Food-contact grade only; letter of compliance from masterbatch supplier
- Processing lubricants: Food-grade lubricants (NSF H1 or equivalent) required in Zone B (moulding)
- Cleaning agents: Food-grade (NAFDAC-listed) for PET production area

## 6. NCS — Nigeria Customs Service

### 6.1 Import Compliance

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Form M | Mandatory for all imports; opened at an authorised commercial bank |
| HS Code classification | Correct HS codes for PP (3902.10), PE (3901.10/20), PET (3907.61), additives (3812/3824) |
| Import Duty | PP/PE/PET: 5% ECOWAS CET; additives: 5–10% CET; check current rates |
| VAT | 7.5% on all imports |
| CISS | Comprehensive Import Supervision Scheme — Pre-inspection at origin |
| SONCAP | Required at origin for regulated plastics and chemicals |
| NAFDAC import permit | Required for food-contact raw materials |
| Import prohibition check | Verify goods are not on CBN/NCS import prohibition list |

### 6.2 Export Compliance (for CCH-PLS finished goods supplied to West African customers)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Export documentation | Commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin (NAFDAC/SON) |
| ECOWAS TLS | Trade Liberalisation Scheme for intra-ECOWAS trade — may attract duty waiver |
| Export permit | Not required for most plastics; check NEPZA/NESREA for any controlled items |

## 9. Health & Safety Requirements

### 9.1 Nigerian Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Applicability |
|------------|---------------|
| Factories Act (Cap F1 LFN 2004) | Registration as a factory; annual inspection |
| Employee Compensation Act 2010 | Mandatory employer's liability insurance |
| NSITF | Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund — mandatory contributions |
| PENCOM | Pension Fund — mandatory contributions |
| ITF | Industrial Training Fund levy |
| Lagos State Environmental Law | Workplace noise, waste, chemical storage |

### 9.2 Key Process Safety Requirements

| Hazard | Control Measure |
|--------|----------------|
| High-temperature polymer melt (up to 300 °C) | Machine guarding, SIS high-temperature ESD, PPE (heat-resistant gloves, face shields) |
| Hot polymer stringing / splash | Machine nozzle covers, burn treatment station, PPE |
| High-pressure hydraulics (injection machines) | Pressure relief valves, SIS interlocks, regular hydraulic hose inspection |
| Compressed air release | Pressure vessel certification (ASME/PED), safety relief valves |
| Polymer dust and fume | LEV (local exhaust ventilation) at all extruder vents; respiratory PPE |
| Solvent and chemical handling | SDS training; chemical-resistant PPE; ventilated hazmat zone |
| Manual handling (heavy moulds, IBCs) | Overhead crane, forklift, IBC trolleys; manual handling training |
| Noise (extruders > 85 dB) | Hearing protection zones marked; audiometric testing for exposed workers |
| Electrical safety | Lock-out tag-out (LOTO) procedure; annual electrical inspection |
