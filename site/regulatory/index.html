    Regulatory & Compliance¶

    Factory: Coo-Cah Plastics & Polymers Factory (CCH-PLS) Document: Regulatory Compliance Register v1.0 Status: PLANNED — Permits and certifications to be obtained before production Master Repo Reference: Coo-Kah-Doks / docs / iso-standards.md

    1. Regulatory Landscape Summary¶

    Operating a polymer/plastics manufacturing facility in Nigeria involves oversight from six distinct regulatory authorities, each with specific permit and compliance requirements.

    Authority Full Name Scope
    NESREA National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency Environmental permits, hazardous materials, effluent discharge
    NUPRC (formerly DPR) Nigerian Upstream Petroleum Regulatory Commission Hydrocarbon feedstock handling permits
    SON Standards Organisation of Nigeria Product quality certification, SONCAP import certificates
    NAFDAC National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control Food-contact plastic products (PET bottles, food packaging)
    NCS Nigeria Customs Service Import documentation (Form M), tariff classification
    Lagos State Fire Service / NFPA Lagos State Fire Service Fire safety compliance for chemical and industrial storage

    2. NESREA — Environmental Compliance¶

    2.1 Required Permits¶

    Permit Requirement Frequency Status
    Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Mandatory before construction; Schedule II activity under NESREA Act Once (with annual review) ☐ Not started
    Waste Discharge Permit For effluent discharge from ETP to public drains / water body Annual renewal ☐ Not started
    Air Emissions Permit For VOC/fume emissions from extrusion processes Annual renewal ☐ Not started
    Hazardous Waste Generation Licence Required to generate, transport, and dispose of hazardous plastic process waste Annual renewal ☐ Not started
    NESREA Facility Registration Register as an industrial facility Once ☐ Not started

    2.2 Ongoing NESREA Compliance Requirements¶

    Requirement Standard Frequency
    Effluent discharge monitoring NESREA National Environmental (Effluent Limitations) Regulations 2011 Monthly sampling + quarterly NESREA report
    Air quality monitoring NESREA National Environmental (Air Quality Control) Regulations 2014 Quarterly
    Hazardous waste manifest NESREA — for all hazardous waste movements off-site Per event
    Annual environmental report Self-monitoring report submitted to NESREA Annual
    Emergency response plan NESREA requires documented emergency response for chemical facilities Annual review

    2.3 Key Environmental Parameters (ETP Discharge Limits)¶

    Parameter NESREA Limit Internal Target Monitoring
    pH 6.0 – 9.0 6.5 – 8.5 Daily
    COD ≤ 40 mg/L ≤ 30 mg/L Weekly
    BOD₅ ≤ 30 mg/L ≤ 20 mg/L Weekly
    Total Suspended Solids (TSS) ≤ 30 mg/L ≤ 20 mg/L Weekly
    Oil and Grease ≤ 10 mg/L ≤ 5 mg/L Weekly
    Temperature ≤ 40 °C ≤ 35 °C Continuous

    3. NUPRC (formerly DPR) — Hydrocarbon Handling¶

    Requirement Detail Status
    Facility permit for hydrocarbon feedstock Required for storage/use of propylene, naphtha, and polymer intermediates from Dangote ☐ Not started
    Petroleum Products Storage Permit For diesel generator fuel tank (≥ 1,000 L) ☐ Not started
    SPCC plan (Spill Prevention, Control and Countermeasure) Documented spill response for hydrocarbon materials ☐ Not started
    NUPRC inspection Pre-commissioning inspection for hydrocarbon handling areas ☐ Not started

    4. SON — Standards Organisation of Nigeria¶

    4.1 Product Certification Requirements¶

    Product (SKU) SON Standard Certification Required Status
    CCH-PLS-003 (PP/PE film) NIS 375 (Plastic film) SON product certification ☐ Not started
    CCH-PLS-004 (PET bottles) NIS 40 (Plastic containers for food) SON product certification + food contact ☐ Not started
    CCH-PLS-006 (HDPE pipes) NIS 115 (PE pipes for water supply) SON mark of conformity ☐ Not started
    CCH-PLS-007 (Crates/containers) NIS / ASTM equivalent SON certification ☐ Not started
    CCH-PLS-001/002 (Injection mouldings) IEC 60335 compliance (for appliance housings) SON + OEM compliance ☐ Not started

    4.2 SONCAP (SON Conformity Assessment Programme)¶

    All polymer raw materials imported must carry SONCAP certificates obtained at the point of origin (pre-shipment inspection). This applies to: - PP/PE granule imports (backup source) - PET resin imports - Specialty additive imports

    5. NAFDAC — Food-Contact Plastic Products¶

    5.1 NAFDAC Scope¶

    NAFDAC regulates all plastic products that come into contact with food or beverages: - CCH-PLS-004 — PET bottles and preforms (food and beverage contact) - CCH-PLS-003 — PP/PE blown film for food packaging

    5.2 NAFDAC Registration Requirements¶

    Requirement Detail Status
    Manufacturer registration Register factory as a food-packaging manufacturer with NAFDAC ☐ Not started
    Product registration Each food-contact plastic product requires individual NAFDAC registration ☐ Not started
    Raw material declaration List of all raw materials, additives, and process aids submitted to NAFDAC ☐ Not started
    Migration testing Heavy metals and additive migration testing per EU 10/2011 or FDA 21 CFR ☐ Not started
    Acetaldehyde (AA) testing PET bottles — AA must be < 3 ppm (NAFDAC food contact requirement) ☐ Not started
    Facility inspection NAFDAC factory inspection before product registration approval ☐ Not started
    Annual renewal NAFDAC registration renewal Annual

    5.3 Key Food-Contact Raw Material Requirements¶

    For NAFDAC-compliant food-contact production: - PET resin: Must have food-contact declaration from supplier (FDA/EFSA compliance) - Masterbatch: Food-contact grade only; letter of compliance from masterbatch supplier - Processing lubricants: Food-grade lubricants (NSF H1 or equivalent) required in Zone B (moulding) - Cleaning agents: Food-grade (NAFDAC-listed) for PET production area

    6. NCS — Nigeria Customs Service¶

    6.1 Import Compliance¶

    Requirement Detail
    Form M Mandatory for all imports; opened at an authorised commercial bank
    HS Code classification Correct HS codes for PP (3902.10), PE (3901.10/20), PET (3907.61), additives (3812/3824)
    Import Duty PP/PE/PET: 5% ECOWAS CET; additives: 5–10% CET; check current rates
    VAT 7.5% on all imports
    CISS Comprehensive Import Supervision Scheme — Pre-inspection at origin
    SONCAP Required at origin for regulated plastics and chemicals
    NAFDAC import permit Required for food-contact raw materials
    Import prohibition check Verify goods are not on CBN/NCS import prohibition list

    6.2 Export Compliance (for CCH-PLS finished goods supplied to West African customers)¶

    Requirement Detail
    Export documentation Commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin (NAFDAC/SON)
    ECOWAS TLS Trade Liberalisation Scheme for intra-ECOWAS trade — may attract duty waiver
    Export permit Not required for most plastics; check NEPZA/NESREA for any controlled items

    Requirement Standard Status
    Fire safety plan submission Lagos State Fire Safety Law ☐ Not started
    Lagos State Fire Service inspection Pre-occupancy inspection ☐ Not started
    Fire detection system (installed + certified) NFPA 72 / BS 5839 ☐ Not started
    Fire suppression (sprinkler / clean agent) NFPA 13 (sprinkler) / NFPA 2001 (clean agent) ☐ Not started
    Fire hydrant ring main NFPA 24 ☐ Not started
    Portable extinguishers NFPA 10 ☐ Not started
    Emergency lighting and exit signage NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code) ☐ Not started
    Hazmat chemical storage classification NFPA 704 diamond system ☐ Not started
    Fire escape routes (min. 2) NFPA 101 ☐ Not started
    Fire drill record Twice per year minimum ☐ Not started
    Annual fire safety inspection Lagos State Fire Service Annual

    9. Health & Safety Requirements¶

    9.1 Nigerian Regulatory Framework¶

    Regulation Applicability
    Factories Act (Cap F1 LFN 2004) Registration as a factory; annual inspection
    Employee Compensation Act 2010 Mandatory employer's liability insurance
    NSITF Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund — mandatory contributions
    PENCOM Pension Fund — mandatory contributions
    ITF Industrial Training Fund levy
    Lagos State Environmental Law Workplace noise, waste, chemical storage

    9.2 Key Process Safety Requirements¶

    Hazard Control Measure
    High-temperature polymer melt (up to 300 °C) Machine guarding, SIS high-temperature ESD, PPE (heat-resistant gloves, face shields)
    Hot polymer stringing / splash Machine nozzle covers, burn treatment station, PPE
    High-pressure hydraulics (injection machines) Pressure relief valves, SIS interlocks, regular hydraulic hose inspection
    Compressed air release Pressure vessel certification (ASME/PED), safety relief valves
    Polymer dust and fume LEV (local exhaust ventilation) at all extruder vents; respiratory PPE
    Solvent and chemical handling SDS training; chemical-resistant PPE; ventilated hazmat zone
    Manual handling (heavy moulds, IBCs) Overhead crane, forklift, IBC trolleys; manual handling training
    Noise (extruders > 85 dB) Hearing protection zones marked; audiometric testing for exposed workers
    Electrical safety Lock-out tag-out (LOTO) procedure; annual electrical inspection

    10. Compliance Calendar¶

    Month Action Required
    Pre-construction EIA application to NESREA; Fire safety plan submission
    Pre-construction NUPRC facility permit application
    Pre-commissioning NESREA inspection; fire service inspection
    Pre-commissioning NCS/SONCAP supplier registration
    Commissioning NAFDAC factory inspection (food-contact lines)
    Month 1 (production) Effluent discharge permit; air emissions permit
    Month 3 SON product certification applications
    Month 6 IMS documentation complete; internal audits begin
    Month 12 Stage 1 + Stage 2 ISO 9001/14001/45001 certification audits
    Month 18 ISO 50001 certification audit
    Annual NESREA annual report; fire service inspection; ISO surveillance audits
